def average(trials, width=5):
    avgp = np.copy(p)
    for i in range(trials):
        avgp = np.convolve(p, avgp)
    plt.xlabel("average of 10 dice")
    plt.ylabel("probability")
    pofavg = np.linspace(1, 6, len(avgp))
    plt.vlines(pofavg, 0, avgp, linewidth = 5);
    plt.xlim(1, 6)
    plt.ylim(0, None)
    return pofavg, avgp
average(10);

The average does follow a gaussian distribution. There are peak and wing pattern point it to a gaussian. It will be a better approximation when the sample size increase.
